About

I'm obsessed with data. Not just collecting it-understanding what it's trying to tell us. For the past four years, I built high-performance data pipelines in industry, optimizing for speed and reliability. Now, as a Master's student and Research Assistant III in Professor Matthew Colonnese's lab at GWU's School of Medicine & Health Sciences, I'm tackling a more interesting challenge: decoding neural signals.

Every day, I work with raw neurophysiology data-thousands of electrodes recording neurons firing. My job is to build the infrastructure and machine learning models that transform this noise into signal. I design scalable Python-based pipelines, implement spike sorting algorithms, and develop classification models that help neuroscientists understand how the brain encodes information. It's data engineering meets neuroscience, and it's the most meaningful work I've done.

The intersection I'm exploring: How can we apply production-grade data architecture (Python, Spark, Kafka, AWS) and modern ML techniques (neural networks, clustering algorithms) to unlock insights in neuroscience that would otherwise be impossible to extract?

My work focuses on infrastructure, automation, and reliable execution of complex neural data workflows rather than developing new spike-detection algorithms.
